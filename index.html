Good questions

Frequently asked

Is the AI really free?
Yes. Daybook's default assistant is a small language model that runs entirely on your phone — no account, no API key, no cost, and it works offline. If you'd rather use a frontier cloud model, you can add your own provider key; that's the only part that ever uses the network, and it goes straight from your device to your provider.
Do I need an account?
No. There's no sign-up and no server. You install the app and it works. Nothing syncs to us, because there's no "us" in the loop.
Does it work offline?
Yes — the calendar, the on-device AI, the morning brief and natural-language entry all work with no connection. The only network use is optional: syncing your existing calendars, or an AI cloud key if you choose to add one.
Which calendars does it use?
The ones already on your device — Google, Samsung, Outlook or local. Daybook reads and writes them in place, so there's nothing to migrate.
Is my data really private?
Your calendar, contacts and notes are processed and stored on your device. There's no server, no analytics, and no ad or tracking SDKs — in the app or on this website. Any API keys you add are kept in the Android Keystore.
When is iOS coming?
It's in TestFlight now and the App Store release is coming soon. Daybook is one app across phones, foldables and tablets.
