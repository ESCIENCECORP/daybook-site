What's inside

Everything in your life, in one private place

Six things a plain calendar can't do — all running on your own phone.

Free AI on your phone

The built-in assistant runs entirely on your device — no account, no API key, no cost, even offline. It writes your morning brief and understands plain language.

Screenshot → event

Someone proposed a time in a chat? Scan the screenshot. Every date is found on-device, you pick the right slot, then confirm. The image never leaves your phone.

Every account

Works directly on the calendars already on your device — Google, Samsung, Outlook or local — with month, week, day and list views and drag-to-reschedule.

Remembers people

Contacts become people: birthdays appear automatically, each person has a timeline of calls and notes, and Daybook nudges you when you've drifted apart.

Promises, kept

“I'll send it by Friday.” Daybook detects commitments in your notes, asks you to confirm, and tracks each one until it's kept — before it slips.

Type it like you say it

“Lunch with Bob tomorrow 12pm.” Daybook previews exactly what will change, applies it only when you confirm, and every change can be undone.

A proactive Today page

Your morning, briefed before you ask

Every morning Daybook reads your day and tells you what matters — written by the AI on your own silicon.

  • Travel-buffer warnings between venues that are too far apart
  • Birthdays coming up with nothing planned yet
  • People you've drifted from, by your own usual cadence
  • Tasks due and unresolved promises, surfaced early

Private by design

The selling point isn't a feature. It's that nothing leaves your phone.

No account. No server. No analytics. Your data stays in your device's own calendar and contacts stores.

No server, ever

The whole app is bundled into the install and works with zero backend. The default install sends nothing, anywhere.

AI on your silicon

Intelligence runs on-device by default. Prefer a frontier model? Bring your own key — requests go straight from your phone to your provider.

Your keys, your control

Any API keys you add are stored in the Android Keystore. Every AI-written text is labeled with where it ran — on-device or cloud.
